Aaron Kirtland

Research Experiences

Participant, Research in Industrial Projects for Students (RIPS) at the Institute for Pure and Applied Mathematics (IPAM) at UCLA

Summer 2020

Implemented, optimized, and analyzed a denoising algorithm for signals arising from chaotic dynamical systems. Research was performed in a group of four for the Air Force Research Laboratory. Used Python to test the algorithm on data from Hall Effect Thrusters.

Participant, Complexity Across Disciplines REU at Boise State University

Summer 2019

Researched number theoretic and algebraic properties of Ring-LWE-based post-quantum cryptosystems in a team of four under Dr. Liljana Babinkostova. Focused on bounding the spectral norm of a class of matrices used in the RLWE cryptosystem.
